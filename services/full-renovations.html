Common Questions

House renovations in Cork — the questions we’re asked most.

How much does a house renovation cost in Cork?

There’s no single price — a renovation is quoted per square metre after we’ve surveyed the house, and the figure moves with the scope: how much structural work is involved, the level of finish, and whether you’re extending. Rather than a headline number that means little, we give you a clear, itemised quote once we’ve walked the house with you.

How long does a full house renovation take?

It depends on the size of the house and the scope of the works, so we set a realistic timeline after the survey rather than promising a fixed number up front. Because one in-house team runs the whole job, there are fewer gaps between trades, which keeps the programme tighter than a job passed between separate subcontractors.

Do I need planning permission to renovate my house?

Many internal renovations don’t need planning permission, but extensions over a certain size, structural changes and work to protected structures often do. We assess this early and handle any planning application as part of the project, so you’re not left to work it out on your own.

Can I live in the house during the renovation?

For a full house renovation it’s usually not practical to stay in the house while the work is underway, though it depends on the scope. We talk through the timeline and any phasing with you at the consultation so you can plan around it.

Which parts of Cork do you cover?

We work across Cork city and county, including Douglas, Ballincollig, Carrigaline, Blackrock, Glanmire and Bishopstown, along with the surrounding towns and villages. If you’re in the wider Cork area, it’s worth asking.

Will you handle the SEAI grant paperwork?

Yes. As part of a deep retrofit we help you access SEAI grant support — guiding the assessment, the forms and the approvals — and the funding is factored into your quote up front rather than chased afterwards.
