Common Questions

Home renovation grants in Cork — the questions we’re asked most.

What grants can I get for doing up a house in Cork?

Broadly, two kinds. SEAI home-energy grants support improving your home’s energy rating — insulation, windows, solar, a heat pump and so on — and the council runs grants for vacant and derelict homes, such as the Vacant Property Refurbishment Grant. Which ones apply depends on your property and your plans, and we help you work that out at the consultation.

What is the vacant property or derelict property grant?

It’s a council-run grant (the Croí Cónaithe Vacant Property Refurbishment Grant) that supports doing up a home that has been vacant for a period, with extra support where a property is on the derelict register. It’s administered by Cork City or Cork County Council, and we help you understand it and pull the documentation together as part of your project. The amounts and eligibility are set by the council, so we point you to the current detail.

How does TRC help with grants?

We guide you and fold the grant side into your project — we don’t administer or approve grants ourselves. In practice that means we explain what’s available, help you see what you qualify for, and work with a registered grant agent who runs the SEAI application process, while we handle the building work and build any confirmed funding into your quote. TRC is a builder that guides you through grants, not a grant provider.

How do SEAI energy grants work?

SEAI grants are tied to improving your home’s energy rating, so they usually go hand in hand with a retrofit — upgrading insulation, airtightness, windows and heating to bring the house up toward a B2. You need approval before the work starts, which is why we sort the grant side up front. The application itself is run by a registered grant agent we work with, and the energy upgrade is set out on our home retrofit page.

Can the grant be folded into my renovation quote?

Yes. Where support is confirmed, we build it into your quote and handle it as part of the project, so the funding offsets the work rather than being something you chase separately afterwards. Because grant amounts depend on the property and the measures, we keep the figures to your actual quote rather than headline numbers.

Am I eligible?

It depends on the property. Council vacant and derelict grants have their own rules around how long a home has been vacant and when it was built, and SEAI support depends on the home’s current rating and the upgrade planned. Rather than guess, we assess your specific situation at the free consultation and are straight with you about what’s realistic.
